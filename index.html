§ I · Current Focus
The next decade of security belongs to those who can prove, not merely assume, that a system is safe.

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and cryptography — two fields whose conventional assumptions are being quietly overturned. I study how learning systems fail under adversarial pressure, and how provable cryptographic primitives can be composed into protocols that withstand those failures at scale.

The research programme I'm pursuing addresses a pointed question: as machine learning becomes infrastructure, what are the minimum cryptographic guarantees we can demand of it, and at what cost? This is the throughline across my publications, my applied work with anomaly-detection pipelines, and the thesis direction I'm pursuing into doctoral study.
